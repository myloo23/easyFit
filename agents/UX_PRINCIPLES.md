# UX Principles

## Product experience

- Prefer clarity over novelty.
- Make uncertainty visible.
- Explain derived metrics without overwhelming the user.
- Show source and freshness where it matters.
- Let users drill from insight to evidence.
- Avoid medical language unless clinically appropriate and legally reviewed.

## Analytics presentation

- Label measured values, calculated metrics, estimates, predictions, and recommendations differently.
- Explain personal baselines.
- Show confidence and limitations for important metrics.
- Avoid false precision.
- Make missing data explicit.

## Early UX scope

Phase 1 UX can be minimal and diagnostic. Its job is to support local Apple Health export inventory, safe data availability inspection, and later localhost dashboard decisions, not a polished consumer dashboard.
