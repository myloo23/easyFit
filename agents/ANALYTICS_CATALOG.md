# Analytics Catalog

This catalog records future analytics areas. It is intentionally not a formula sheet yet.

## Recovery

Possible inputs:

- HRV.
- Resting heart rate.
- Sleep duration and quality.
- Recent training load.
- Respiratory trends.
- Illness or stress signals if legitimately available and justified.

Current status: research backlog. Do not invent a score yet.

## Sleep

Potential analysis:

- Duration.
- Stages.
- Efficiency.
- Fragmentation.
- Consistency.
- Relationship with recovery and workout performance.

Current status: depends on HealthKit sleep availability and source quality.

## Training load

Research areas:

- Duration-based load.
- Heart-rate-based models.
- TRIMP-related methods.
- Power-based load.
- Short-term and long-term load.
- Monotony and strain concepts.

Current status: research backlog. Avoid proprietary formulas and unexplained scoring.

## Running workout analysis

Potential analysis:

- Pace.
- Heart rate.
- Power.
- Cadence.
- Running dynamics.
- Elevation.
- Splits.
- Route-aware context.

Current status: requires Phase 4 workout reconstruction.

## Cardiac drift

Research robust methods for comparing heart rate response to stable effort over time. Account for terrain, heat, hydration, fatigue, power/pace stability, stops, and workout structure.

Current status: research backlog.

## Durability

Potential goal: understand how well the runner maintains performance and mechanics over long workouts.

Possible signals:

- Pace.
- Power.
- Heart rate.
- Cadence.
- Stride length.
- Ground contact time.
- Vertical oscillation.

Current status: requires reliable long-run stream alignment.

## Marathon preparation

Potential areas:

- Weekly volume.
- Consistency.
- Long-run progression.
- Marathon-specific workouts.
- Durability.
- Recovery.
- Taper.
- Race prediction.

Keep theoretical fitness prediction separate from currently supported marathon performance.
