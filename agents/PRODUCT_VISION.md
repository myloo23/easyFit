# Product Vision

easyFit should help athletes understand how training, recovery, sleep, and long-term habits relate to performance.

## Questions the product should eventually answer

- How well am I recovering?
- How is my sleep affecting performance?
- Is my aerobic fitness improving?
- Am I accumulating too much fatigue?
- How stable am I during long runs?
- Where does my running form begin to deteriorate?
- How is my marathon preparation progressing?
- What performance is realistically supported by my current fitness?
- Which personal habits appear related to better or worse performance?

## Product principles

### Transparent metrics

Important derived metrics must explain what they measure, which inputs they use, their methodology, confidence, and limitations.

### Personal baselines

Where appropriate, compare against the user's own history rather than generic population norms.

### Scientific honesty

Clearly distinguish measured data, calculated metrics, estimates, predictions, correlations, and recommendations. Never present correlation as causation.

### Privacy by design

Health data is sensitive. Privacy must influence storage, logging, sync, analytics, third-party integrations, support workflows, and deletion/export design from the start.

### Market readiness

Foundations should not block future multi-user support, App Store distribution, subscriptions, account deletion, data export, or production observability, even if these are not built now.
